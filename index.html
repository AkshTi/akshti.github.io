Self-Supervised Preference Learning for Multimodal Foundation Models

MIT Laboratory for Financial Engineering (LFE) · Jun 2024 – Present · Under Review

A self-supervised post-training method that aligns multimodal foundation models using no human annotations, external judges, or task-specific labels. Trained on unlabeled time-series charts, the resulting models transfer zero-shot to held-out tasks — achieving up to 31% relative gain on financial large-move detection, 14% on crash-risk prediction, and 10% on ECG abnormality detection over a supervised fine-tuning baseline.

Contributions
  • Developed a self-supervised preference construction pipeline requiring no labels or human feedback, validated across financial, ECG, and seismic domains.
  • Ran iterative DPO on Qwen2-VL-7B and Gemma-3-4B; gains replicate across both model families and accumulate over training rounds.
  • Probed internal representations to confirm post-training improves visual grounding, not just output fluency.
